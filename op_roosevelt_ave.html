As New York City continues Operation Restore Roosevelt Ave,
data shows sex-work related arrests are at their lowest-point in nearly 20 years.

By: Mia Hollie, Asad Jung, David Paiz-Torres

As a Black, trans, gender-nonconforming person, Victoria Von Blaque felt that Roosevelt Avenue was a safe haven for her. “This area was never unsafe for marginalized communities,” said Von Blaque. However, ever since Mayor Eric Adams launched “Operation Restore Roosevelt,” the area doesn’t offer the same protection it used to, said Von Blaque.

“Operation Restore Roosevelt” is a 90-day-operation launched by the Mayor’s office to target sex work and illegal street vending along Roosevelt Avenue. While many residents of the area are in support of the operation, sex workers and advocates have criticized it, saying it will target already vulnerable transgender and immigrant communities.
